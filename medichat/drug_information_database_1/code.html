Ibuprofen

Description

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used for relieving pain, helping with fever, and reducing inflammation. It works by blocking the production of certain natural substances in your body that cause inflammation.

Uses

  • Relief of mild to moderate pain (e.g., headache, dental pain, menstrual cramps).
  • Reduction of fever.
  • Alleviation of pain and inflammation associated with arthritis.

Side Effects

Common side effects include:

  • Upset stomach, nausea, vomiting, heartburn.
  • Diarrhea, constipation.
  • Dizziness, headache.

Seek medical attention if you experience severe side effects like chest pain, shortness of breath, or signs of an allergic reaction.

Dosage

Adults: 200-400 mg every 4-6 hours as needed. Do not exceed 1200 mg in 24 hours unless directed by a doctor. Dosage for children depends on their age and weight. Always follow the instructions on the label or from your healthcare provider.

Interactions

Ibuprofen may interact with other medications, such as aspirin, blood thinners, and other NSAIDs. Inform your doctor about all medications you are taking.

warning

Warnings

Do not use if you have ever had an allergic reaction to any other pain reliever/fever reducer. Avoid use if you have stomach bleeding or ulcers. Long-term use may increase the risk of heart attack or stroke.
